The Pucci extremal operators

Published

30 November 2022

Let $\Omega\subset \mathbb{R^n}$ be an open subset with smooth boundary. We consider the following second-order elliptic equation in the nondivergence form

\begin{equation}\label{eq:main-1} \sum_{i,j=1}^n a_{ij}(x)\partial_{ij}v(x) = 0 \qquad\text{in}\;\Omega \tag{1} \end{equation}

under the uniform ellipticity assumption

\begin{equation}\label{eq:main-2} \lambda \mathbb{I} \preceq \big( a_{ij}(x) \big)_{ij} \preceq \Lambda \mathbb{I} \qquad\text{for all}\;x\in \Omega \tag{2} \end{equation}

Here I\mathbb{I}I is the identity n×nn\times nn×n matrix. We write A(x)=(aij(x))ij\mathbb{A}(x) = \big( a_{ij}(x)\big)_{ij}A(x)=(aij​(x))ij​ for simplicity.

The main classical result on equation \eqref{eq:main-1} is the DeGiorgi-Nash-Moser’s Holder estimate. No other assumption on (aij(x))\big(a_{ij}(x)\big)(aij​(x)) is needed, so they can be discontinuous. To formulate \eqref{eq:main-1} in the viscosity solution framework if (aij(x))\big(a_{ij}(x)\big)(aij​(x)) is discontinuous, we use the Pucci extremal operators.
